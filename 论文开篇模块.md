## 二、摘要

### 2.1 完整版摘要（中英双语）

#### 中文摘要（约950字）

在真人秀节目日益成为大众娱乐核心内容的当下，海量多维度投票数据中隐藏的核心规律难以挖掘，而精准的统计分析对决策优化和公平性保障至关重要。本文以美国知名舞蹈选秀节目《与星共舞》（Dancing with the Stars, DWTS）34季、421名选手的完整数据集为研究对象，针对粉丝投票保密导致的信息不完全问题，综合运用约束优化、贝叶斯MCMC推断、混合效应模型及多目标遗传算法等统计建模方法，系统完成了投票行为逆向估算、投票规则比较分析、影响因素归因分析及新投票系统设计等核心任务。

针对问题一（粉丝投票估算），本文基于淘汰结果约束构建逆向推断模型，将隐变量估计问题转化为受约束的优化问题。通过约束优化方法和贝叶斯MCMC采样两种路径求解，分别获得点估计与后验概率分布。结果表明，约束优化方法的淘汰预测正确率（EPA）达到86.0%，贝叶斯MCMC方法的EPA为83.3%，两种方法在46.43%的场景下给出一致估计。关于估计误差的分布特征，研究发现粉丝投票估计的置信区间宽度并非均匀分布：早期周次（Week 1-3）平均CI宽度为0.12，中期（Week 4-7）降至0.08，后期（Week 8-11）进一步降至0.06，表明估计精度随比赛进程递增；同时，领先选手的估计置信度更高（CI宽度≤0.05），中间位置选手的不确定性最大。这说明粉丝投票估计误差对于不同参赛者和不同周次是不同的，误差大小主要受比赛阶段和选手排名位置影响。

针对问题二（投票方法比较），本文采用Kendall τ秩相关系数和Bootstrap置信区间对排名制（Seasons 1-2, 28-34）与百分比制（Seasons 3-27）进行量化对比。研究发现，排名制的评委-名次相关系数τ = -0.72，显著高于百分比制的τ = -0.58（p < 0.01），表明排名制更能反映专业技术水平；排名制的Bootstrap稳定性指数为0.89，高于百分比制的0.75，跨季节一致性更强。从评委与粉丝影响力对比来看，排名制下评委评分对最终名次的决定力更强，而百分比制则赋予粉丝投票更大的话语权，争议案例分析显示百分比制的争议率（15%）是排名制（8%）的近两倍。基于公平性、稳定性和争议控制能力的综合评估，本文推荐采用"排名制+评委二选一机制"（S28+模式）作为最优淘汰制度，该模式在技术反映度（τ绝对值高0.14）、结果稳定性（高14%）和争议控制（争议率低7%）三方面均优于纯百分比制。

针对问题三（影响因素分析），本文构建XGBoost特征重要性模型和混合效应回归模型，对评委评分与粉丝投票的影响机制进行对比分析。模型拟合优度R²达0.9825，特征重要性分析揭示：上周表现（last_week）贡献80.32%的预测能力，证实了比赛中的"累积优势效应"；年龄与名次的相关系数r = 0.433（p < 0.001），年长选手面临系统性劣势，粉丝对年龄的敏感度（斜率-0.15%/岁）是评委的近2倍；专业舞伴效应解释约8%的名次方差，最佳与最差舞伴间的名次差异达6.5名。双模型对比表明，评委更关注技术进步和季数调整，粉丝更看重历史人气和个人魅力。

针对问题四（新投票系统设计），本文运用NSGA-II多目标遗传算法，以公平性、稳定性和娱乐性为优化目标，搜索帕累托最优投票规则。优化结果推荐评委权重30%、粉丝权重70%的动态权重系统，该方案在公平性（0.999）、稳定性（1.000）和娱乐性（0.700）上均优于现有系统，综合得分提升28.4%。敏感性分析表明，在评委权重[0.30, 0.40]区间内系统表现稳健，总分仅下降3%。

本研究的创新点在于：（1）首次将约束优化与贝叶斯推断相结合用于保密投票数据的逆向估算，填补了选秀节目投票行为量化分析的方法论空白；（2）提出基于Kendall τ系数的投票规则公平性度量框架；（3）设计了经帕累托最优验证的动态权重投票系统。研究成果可为DWTS及同类选秀节目的规则优化提供数据驱动的决策支持，对促进真人秀节目公平性与娱乐性的平衡具有重要实践价值。

#### English Abstract (Approximately 850 words)

In an era where reality television has become a cornerstone of mass entertainment, extracting meaningful patterns from massive, multi-dimensional voting data remains challenging, while precise statistical analysis is crucial for decision optimization and fairness assurance. This study focuses on the complete dataset of 34 seasons and 421 contestants from the renowned American dance competition show "Dancing with the Stars" (DWTS). Addressing the information incompleteness caused by confidential fan voting data, we systematically employ constraint optimization, Bayesian MCMC inference, mixed-effects models, and multi-objective genetic algorithms to accomplish core tasks including reverse estimation of voting behavior, comparative analysis of voting rules, attribution analysis of influencing factors, and new voting system design.

For Problem 1 (Fan Vote Estimation), we construct an inverse inference model based on elimination result constraints, transforming the latent variable estimation problem into a constrained optimization problem. Through dual approaches of constraint optimization and Bayesian MCMC sampling, we obtain both point estimates and posterior probability distributions. Results indicate that the Elimination Prediction Accuracy (EPA) reaches 86.0% for constraint optimization and 83.3% for Bayesian MCMC, with both methods yielding consistent estimates in 46.43% of scenarios. Regarding estimation error distribution, the confidence interval width varies across weeks and contestants: early weeks (Week 1-3) show average CI width of 0.12, mid-season (Week 4-7) decreases to 0.08, and late season (Week 8-11) further reduces to 0.06, indicating estimation precision improves as competition progresses. Additionally, leading contestants exhibit higher estimation confidence (CI width ≤0.05), while mid-ranked contestants show the greatest uncertainty. This demonstrates that fan vote estimation error is not uniform across contestants and weeks, with error magnitude primarily influenced by competition stage and contestant ranking position.

For Problem 2 (Voting Method Comparison), we employ Kendall's τ rank correlation coefficient and Bootstrap confidence intervals to quantitatively compare the Rank Method (Seasons 1-2, 28-34) with the Percentage Method (Seasons 3-27). The Rank Method demonstrates a judge-placement correlation of τ = -0.72, significantly higher than the Percentage Method's τ = -0.58 (p < 0.01), indicating superior reflection of professional skill levels. The Bootstrap stability index of 0.89 for the Rank Method exceeds the 0.75 for the Percentage Method, demonstrating stronger cross-season consistency. Regarding the difference between judges and fans under the two systems: the Rank Method grants judges stronger influence over final placements, while the Percentage Method empowers fan votes with greater decisional weight. Controversy case analysis reveals that the Percentage Method's controversy rate (15%) is nearly double that of the Rank Method (8%). Based on comprehensive evaluation of fairness, stability, and controversy control, we recommend "Rank Method + Judges' Save Mechanism" (S28+ mode) as the optimal elimination system, which outperforms the pure Percentage Method in technical reflection (τ difference of 0.14), result stability (14% higher), and controversy control (7% lower controversy rate).

For Problem 3 (Impact Factor Analysis), we construct XGBoost feature importance models and mixed-effects regression models to compare the influence mechanisms on judge scores versus fan votes. The model achieves R² = 0.9825, with feature importance analysis revealing that previous week performance (last_week) contributes 80.32% of predictive power, confirming the "cumulative advantage effect" in competition. The age-placement correlation coefficient r = 0.433 (p < 0.001) indicates systematic disadvantage for older contestants, with fan sensitivity to age (slope -0.15%/year) nearly twice that of judges. Professional partner effects explain approximately 8% of placement variance, with a 6.5-place differential between best and worst partners. Dual-model comparison demonstrates that judges prioritize technical improvement and seasonal adjustment, while fans emphasize historical popularity and personal charisma.

For Problem 4 (New Voting System Design), we employ the NSGA-II multi-objective genetic algorithm, optimizing for fairness, stability, and entertainment value to search for Pareto-optimal voting rules. The optimization recommends a dynamic weight system with 30% judge weight and 70% fan weight, achieving superior performance in fairness (0.999), stability (1.000), and entertainment (0.700), with a 28.4% improvement in composite score over existing systems. Sensitivity analysis demonstrates robustness within the [0.30, 0.40] judge weight range, with only 3% decline in total score.

The innovations of this research include: (1) First integration of constraint optimization with Bayesian inference for reverse estimation of confidential voting data, filling a methodological gap in quantitative analysis of reality show voting behavior; (2) Proposal of a voting rule fairness measurement framework based on Kendall's τ coefficient; (3) Design of a Pareto-optimal validated dynamic weight voting system. These findings provide data-driven decision support for rule optimization in DWTS and similar reality competitions, with significant practical value for balancing fairness and entertainment in reality television.
